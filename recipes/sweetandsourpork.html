Sweet and Sour Pork Recipe

Description

Sweet and sour pork sits among some of the most well-known Chinese dishes outside of China. It’s widely believed to be a Cantonese dish, known colloquially as gú lóuh yuhk, which roughly translates to “ancient pork”. -madewithlau.com

Ingredients

Main Ingredients

  • 400g Pork (scotch, shoulder, leg)
  • 40g of each Red, Yellow and Green Pepper (capsicums)
  • 40g Red Onion
  • 50g - 200g Pineapple (amount depends on your preference)

Marinade for the Pork (at least 30min)

  • 1.5 tsp garlic salt
  • Half an egg
  • 1.5 tbsp light soy sauce
  • 3 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 4 tbsp cornstarch

Sauce

  • 5 tbsp ketchup
  • 5 tbsp brown sugar
  • 4 tbsp vinegar
  • 3 tbsp salt
  • 1 tsp cornstarch
  • 0.5 tsp salt

Steps

    Step 1: Marinate the pork

  1. Slice the pork up into small even sized pieces to your preference
  2. Mix the sauces for the marinade and massage into the pork in a bowl
  3. Crack, beat and then mix and massage the egg into the pork with the sauce marinade
  4. Let the marinate work and start on the next step

    Step 2: Prepare the vegetables

  1. Cut bell peppers/capsicums into small sizes
  2. Also cut Pineapple into small slices

    Step 3: Mix the sauce and coat the pork

  1. Mix the sauce ingredients listed earlier thoroughly
  2. Coat and massage the marinated pork evenly with flour, a little bit of water may help
  3. Coat the flour coated pork with cornstarch

    Step 4: Fry the pork

  1. Cook the pork when the oil is at 150 degrees - 175 degrees for about 7 mintues, then take it out to rest for 1 - 2 minutes.
  2. Heat the oil to 200 degrees before refrying the rested pork for 1 - 2 minutes.
  3. Remove from cooking

    Step 5: Stir fry everything

  1. Add and cook the vegetables in a wok or pan for 30 seconds
  2. Add the sliced Pineapple and cook for another 30 seconds
  3. Remove the vegetables and Pineapple
  4. Add a generous amount of oil and the sauce mix and let it reduce for a bit until it boils
  5. Add pork, vegetables and Pineapple back into the wok/pan making sure to coat the pork well with the reduced sauce for a few minutes
  6. Remove and serve while hot
